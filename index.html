Thin Lens

Thin lens camera is the alternative camera other than perspective camera. The thin lens camera has the adjustable aperture radius and focal distance, which enables depth of field effects and enhances the realism of our scene.
The aperture radius is the size of the lens opening which controls how much light enters. A larger aperture results in a shallower depth of field and more blur in out-of-focus areas.
The focal distance descibes at which distance the image is sharp. It defines the focus point with objects closer or farther from it appearing increasingly blurred.
